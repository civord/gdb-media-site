Peter is a passionate drummer and music educator based in Melbourne, teaching personalised drum lessons from his home studio. He holds a Diploma of Music, and he brings the same focus to every student who walks in: build strong foundational skills, and make sure the lesson is something you look forward to all week.

That second part matters more than people expect. Plenty of students have tried an instrument before and drifted away — not because they lacked ability, but because the lessons ground them down. Peter’s answer is to tailor every session to the individual. Complete beginners start with the fundamentals of timing and coordination; experienced players dig into refining technique, feel, and creativity. Nobody gets a photocopy of someone else’s lesson.

Learning an instrument should be both disciplined and fun — solid technique, paired with the music you’re passionate about.

A typical session moves like a bar of music: warm-ups to loosen up, rudiments to build vocabulary, groove development to turn technique into feel, and then songs — the music you actually love, which is what keeps progress structured and inspiring at the same time.

The goal, in the end, is bigger than beats and fills. It’s a student who grows in confidence, stays inspired, and reaches their full potential — whether that means playing along to favourite records at home, joining a band, or just having a corner of the week that’s completely theirs.

How Peter teaches

Three beliefs behind every lesson

Foundations first

Warm-ups, rudiments, and groove work aren’t box-ticking — they’re the vocabulary everything else is built from. Get them right early, patiently, and the rest comes faster.

Your music is the fuel

Every lesson works toward songs the student genuinely loves. Passion isn’t a distraction from discipline — it’s what makes discipline sustainable.

Confidence is the real outcome

Technique serves something bigger: a student who trusts themselves behind the kit, stays inspired, and keeps growing long after the lesson ends.
